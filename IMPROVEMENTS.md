# buildAcademy -- Improvements & Ideas

A running list of things to add or change in the program. Notes, not final copy.

---

## Opening activity: "What do you want?" (clarity & focus)

**Why:** Right now Week 1 opens straight into the big picture and scoping the shared
app. Before any of that, we should run a short activity that gets each person clear on
what *they* personally want out of the program. People who are clear on their own goal
are locked in, ask better questions, and get far more out of every night. People who
drift in without a goal coast and fade by Week 3.

**Where it goes:** Right at the start of Week 1, before "What We're Building" -- ideally
in the 6:00-6:15pm slot, or as the first thing in the build block. Keep it tight
(~10-15 min) so it doesn't eat into building.

**The core question:** *"What do you actually want out of these three weeks?"*
Push past the vague answer ("learn to code with AI") to something concrete and personal.

### Prompts to get clarity (pick a few)
- What's the one app or idea you'd build if you knew you could? (even half-formed)
- Why now? What changed that made you sign up?
- What would make you say "that was 100% worth it" by Week 3?
- What's the thing you keep getting stuck on / the reason you haven't built it yet?
- A year from now, what do you want to be able to do that you can't today?

### Make it stick (locked in)
- Have everyone **write it down** -- one sentence: "By the end of buildAcademy I want to ___."
- Optionally **share out loud** (cameras on, one line each) -- saying it to the group
  raises commitment and lets us tailor examples to real goals.
- Tie it forward: the app we build together is the *vehicle*; their own idea is the
  *destination*. Connect this directly to the SCOPE framework and the
  "Scope your own app" section at the end -- this is the idea they'll scope.

### Possible framing / ties to existing material
- This pairs naturally with the existing message that **deciding what to build** is the
  real skill AI hasn't removed. "What do you want?" is the human-level version of SCOPE's
  *S (Solve)* and *C (Challenge)* applied to the participant themselves.
- Could become a recurring thread: revisit the one-sentence goal at the start of Weeks 2
  and 3 ("are we on track for what you wrote down?").

### Open questions / to decide
- Solo reflection only, or share-out? (share-out is higher commitment but costs time)
- Do we want them to capture it somewhere persistent (a pinned note, the repo, a form)?
- Should facilitators collect the goals beforehand (pre-cohort email) so Night 1 can
  reference real examples instead of running cold?
- Exact time budget -- needs to fit the 6:00-6:15 / 6:15-7:45 schedule without
  squeezing the build.

### Possible name for the activity
- "What do you want?" / "Get clear, get locked in" / "Your North Star"
